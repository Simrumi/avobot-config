# Objection Handling & Risk Reversal

Every prospect has objections. Address them before they kill the sale.

## The 7 Universal Objections

### 1. "I don't have enough time"
**Reframe:** Time invested now saves time later

**Tactics:**
- Show time-to-results ("See changes in 48 hours")
- Break into small, actionable steps
- Offer templates/done-for-you elements
- Demonstrate efficiency gains

**Copy Example:**
> "I get it. You're busy. That's exactly why you need this — it takes just 15 minutes a day, and saves you 5+ hours weekly."

### 2. "I don't have enough money"
**Reframe:** Cost vs. investment; ROI focus

**Tactics:**
- Show the cost of inaction
- Break down per-day/per-use cost
- Compare to alternatives (more expensive)
- Payment plans
- Strong guarantee

**Copy Example:**
> "At $297, that's less than $1 per day for a system that generated $50,000 in new revenue for our beta testers. What's the cost of staying stuck?"

### 3. "It won't work for me/my situation"
**Reframe:** Universality + specific examples

**Tactics:**
- Testimonials from similar people/situations
- Case studies showing before/after
- "Works especially well if..." positioning
- Money-back guarantee

**Copy Example:**
> "We tested this with complete beginners, established businesses, and everything in between. The framework works because it's based on human psychology, not trends."

### 4. "I can figure this out myself"
**Reframe:** Time cost and trial-and-error pain

**Tactics:**
- Calculate hours spent DIY vs. using solution
- Highlight hidden complexity
- Position as "shortcut", not "crutch"
- Mention opportunity cost

**Copy Example:**
> "Sure, you could spend 2 years testing what works. Or you could use a proven system and see results this month. Your time is worth more than $20/hour, right?"

### 5. "I need to think about it"
**Reframe:** Decision paralysis and lost opportunity

**Tactics:**
- Urgency/scarcity (ethical only)
- Limited-time bonus
- "Fast-action" discount
- Remind of pain that persists during "thinking"

**Copy Example:**
> "Every day you wait is another day of [pain]. The price increases tomorrow. But more importantly, every day of delay costs you [specific cost of waiting]."

### 6. "I've tried similar things before"
**Reframe:** This is fundamentally different

**Tactics:**
- Acknowledge past failures
- Explain why this is different (specific mechanism)
- "Missing piece" positioning
- Stronger guarantee due to confidence

**Copy Example:**
> "You've probably tried [common solutions]. Here's why this is different: instead of [old approach], we use [new mechanism] that actually addresses the root cause."

### 7. "What if it doesn't work?"
**Reframe:** Risk reversal

**Tactics:**
- Money-back guarantee (make it strong)
- "Double your money back" (for high confidence)
- Results-based guarantee
- Trial period
- Support/expert access

**Copy Example:**
> "Try it for 30 days. If you don't see measurable results, I'll refund every penny AND send you a $100 Amazon gift card for wasting your time. That's how confident I am."

## Risk Reversal Strategies

### The Guarantee Spectrum

**Weak:** "30-day money-back guarantee"
**Better:** "30-day unconditional guarantee — keep the bonuses even if you refund"
**Strong:** "Double your money back if you don't see results"
**Extreme:** "I'll work with you 1-on-1 until you succeed, or full refund"

### Building Guarantee Credibility
1. Make it specific (not "satisfaction" — what does that mean?)
2. Remove conditions (no "if you show you tried")
3. Extend the timeline (90 days beats 30 days)
4. Add surprise bonuses (keep these even if refund)
5. Make it prominent (don't hide it in fine print)

### FAQ as Objection Handler
Structure FAQ to address objections:

```
Q: "What if I'm not technical?"
A: [Addresses skill objection]

Q: "How is this different from [competitor]?"
A: [Addresses uniqueness objection]

Q: "Can I really do this part-time?"
A: [Addresses time objection]

Q: "What if I need help?"
A: [Addresses support objection]
```

## The 'Yes Set' Technique

Get small agreements before the big ask:

> "Do you want to grow your business? [Yes]
> Do you agree that copywriting is a key skill? [Yes]
> Would having a proven framework save you time? [Yes]
> Then [CTA]"

## Advanced: The 'Even If' Close

Address the objection while making the offer:

> "Even if you've never written copy before...
> Even if you think you're 'not creative'...
> Even if you've failed with other courses...
> This system will work for you because [reason]."
