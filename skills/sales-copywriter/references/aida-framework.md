# AIDA Framework - Attention-Interest-Desire-Action

The classic marketing framework that moves prospects through cognitive stages.

## Structure

### 1. Attention (A)
Grab attention immediately or lose the reader forever.
- **Pattern interrupt**: Break their mental state
- **Curiosity gap**: Open a loop they must close
- **Bold claim**: State something surprising or contrarian
- **Relatable hook**: Mirror their current reality

**Attention Grabbers:**
- Shocking statistics
- Contrarian statements
- Direct questions
- "I made a huge mistake..."
- "Stop doing [common practice]"

### 2. Interest (I)
Build curiosity and engagement once you have attention.
- **Storytelling**: Narratives are more engaging than facts
- **Uniqueness**: What makes this different?
- **Mechanism**: How does it work? (light details)
- **Relatability**: Keep mirroring their situation
- **Education**: Teach something valuable

**Interest Builders:**
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Most people don't realize..."
- "I discovered something strange..."
- "There's a better way..."

### 3. Desire (D)
Make them WANT what you're offering.
- **Transformation**: Paint the picture of life after purchase
- **Specific benefits**: Concrete outcomes, not vague promises
- **Social proof**: Others are getting these results
- **Scarcity/Urgency**: Why act now?
- **Risk reversal**: Remove objections before they form

**Desire Amplifiers:**
- Vivid before/after scenarios
- Specific dollar amounts or time saved
- Testimonials with results
- "Imagine if..." future pacing

### 4. Action (A)
Tell them exactly what to do next.
- **Single CTA**: One clear action
- **Low friction**: Make it easy to say yes
- **Urgency**: Why now, not later?
- **Guarantee**: Risk removal
- **Button copy**: Use action verbs + benefit

**Effective CTA Patterns:**
- "Get [benefit] Now"
- "Start [outcome] Today"
- "Claim Your [offer]"
- "Yes! I Want [result]"

## Best Used For:
- Cold traffic landing pages
- Advertisement copy
- Broader audiences
- Top-of-funnel content

## Key Principles
- **Each stage must lead naturally to the next**
- **Attention is earned in seconds, lost in milliseconds**
- **Desire is built through specificity, not hype**
- **One CTA = more conversions than multiple CTAs**
