# PAS Framework - Problem-Agitate-Solution

The PAS framework is one of the most reliable formulas for high-converting copy.

## Structure

### 1. Problem (P)
Identify and amplify the pain point your prospect is experiencing.
- **Hook**: Start with a relatable, emotionally-charged problem statement
- **Specificity**: Make it concrete and visceral, not abstract
- **Empathy**: Show you understand their struggle deeply
- **Validation**: Confirm this is indeed their problem

**Example Hook:**
> "You're staring at a blank page. The deadline is tomorrow. And you still don't know how to start this sales page."

### 2. Agitate (A)
Twist the knife. Make the problem feel urgent and emotionally painful.
- **Consequences**: What happens if they don't solve this?
- **Emotional impact**: How does this make them feel?
- **Escalation**: Make it worse than they thought
- **Urgency**: Why waiting is costly

**Agitation Patterns:**
- "But it gets worse..."
- "And every day you wait..."
- "Here's what nobody tells you..."
- "The real cost isn't just X—it's Y"

### 3. Solution (S)
Present your product/service as the clear, logical answer.
- **Transition**: Pivot from pain to hope smoothly
- **Introduction**: Present your solution confidently
- **Mechanism**: Briefly explain HOW it works (builds credibility)
- **Benefits**: Focus on outcomes, not features
- **Proof**: Testimonials, data, social proof
- **Offer**: Clear CTA with urgency/scarcity if appropriate

## Best Used For:
- Sales pages for painful problems
- Email sequences
- Landing pages
- Problem-aware audiences

## Key Principles
- **Lead with pain, end with pleasure**
- **Make the agitation 2-3x longer than problem**
- **Solution should feel inevitable**
- **Always include specific proof**
