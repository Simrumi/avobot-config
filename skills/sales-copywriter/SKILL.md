# Sales Copywriter

Write high-converting sales copy using battle-tested frameworks and conversion principles.

## When to Use This Skill

Use for any conversion-focused writing:
- Sales pages (long-form and short-form)
- Landing pages and squeeze pages
- Product descriptions
- Email sequences and autoresponders
- Video Sales Letter (VSL) scripts
- Webinar scripts
- Advertisement copy
- Opt-in pages
- Upsell/downsell pages

## Core Workflow

### 1. Discovery Questions
Before writing, understand:
- **Product**: What are you selling? (features, benefits, mechanism)
- **Audience**: Who is the ideal customer? (pain points, desires, awareness level)
- **Promise**: What specific result/outcome do you deliver?
- **Proof**: What evidence supports your claims? (testimonials, case studies, data)
- **Offer**: What's the price, guarantee, bonuses?
- **Objections**: What reasons would they have for NOT buying?

→ Read [Headlines](references/headlines.md)

### 4. Structure the Body
Follow your chosen framework to build:
- Opening hook
- Problem/promise section
- Solution presentation
- Proof elements (testimonials, case studies, data)
- Offer presentation (price, bonuses, guarantee)
- Objection handling
- Call-to-action

## Writing Principles

### Voice & Tone
- **Conversational**: Write like you talk, not like a textbook
- **Empathetic**: Show you understand their struggle
- **Confident**: Make bold promises you can back up
- **Specific**: Use concrete numbers, not vague generalities

### The "So What" Test
For every feature or claim, ask "So what?" until you reach an emotion:
- Feature → Function → Emotional Benefit → Identity Shift

### Conversion Elements Checklist
- [ ] Specific, benefit-driven headline
- [ ] Strong hook in first 3 sentences
- [ ] Clear problem identification
- [ ] Agitation of pain/consequences
- [ ] Unique solution positioning
- [ ] Proof elements (3+ types)
- [ ] Risk reversal (guarantee)
- [ ] Single, clear CTA
- [ ] Urgency or scarcity (if authentic)
- [ ] Objection handling woven throughout

## Common Patterns

### Opening Hooks That Work
1. **Story**: "Three years ago, I was $50,000 in debt..."
2. **Shocking claim**: "Everything you've learned about copywriting is wrong."
3. **Question**: "Tired of sales pages that don't convert?"
4. **Statistic**: "87% of businesses fail because of this one mistake..."
5. **Contrarian**: "Stop trying to write 'good' copy."

### Transition Phrases
- "But here's the problem..."
- "Here's what nobody tells you..."
- "That all changed when..."
- "I discovered something strange..."
- "The surprising truth is..."
- "Here's the solution..."

### Urgency Without Hype
- "Price increases Friday at midnight"
- "Only 50 spots available"
- "Doors close in 72 hours"
- "This bonus expires in 48 hours"

## Editing for Conversion

### The "Brutal Edit" Checklist
- [ ] Remove every word that doesn't earn its place
- [ ] Replace adverbs with stronger verbs
- [ ] Convert passive voice to active
- [ ] Shorten sentences over 20 words
- [ ] Break up dense paragraphs (2-3 lines max)
- [ ] Add subheadlines every 3-4 paragraphs
- [ ] Bold key benefits and proof points
- [ ] Use bullet points for scanning

### Scannability Optimization
Most people scan first, read second:
- Subheadlines tell the story alone
- Bold text highlights key benefits
- Bullet points break up walls of text
- Short paragraphs create white space
- Images/graphs illustrate key points

## Testing & Iteration

### A/B Testing Priorities
1. Headline (biggest impact)
2. CTA button copy
3. Offer structure
4. Opening hook
5. Social proof placement

### Post-Launch Optimization
- Track scroll depth
- Monitor drop-off points
- Survey non-buyers
- Test one element at a time
- Document learnings for future pages

## Output Format

When writing sales copy, provide:
1. **Framework used** (and why)
2. **Headline options** (top 3-5)
3. **Full copy** (structured with sections)
4. **CTA recommendations** (button copy + placement)
5. **Testing suggestions** (what to A/B test)

Always write copy that could be published as-is, not just outlines or ideas.
